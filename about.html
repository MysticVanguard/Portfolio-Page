Growth

The first project I ever worked on in my computer science career is the bot I created called AquaMarine. I had learned the very basics of Python using a CodeCademy Course and had multiple friends who knew Python and created bots, so I used Aqua to put my knowledge to the test and learn the intermediate skills when coding in Python. The bot's first version was created over the 2021 summer and was ready to leave testing and be public by August, where it quickly grew to hit the 100 server mark in February of 2022. Since then it became verified and sits at over 350 servers one year later, February of 2023, and has had 19 major updates to the code.

February 6, 2023:

My latest project has been my adventures in web design, and include this website and AquaMarine's website. I started my learning in November of 2022, and as of writing this it is currently February of 2023 and I have put a total of 21 hours towards learning how to write code in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, as well as 6 hours towards creating this website.

Overall my knowledge the past two years has grown drastically, from going to knowing absolutely no code to learning Python, PostgreSQL, Java, JavaScript, HTML, and CSS. I will hopefully continue my pursuit in these teaching so that I can learn more advanced computer science ideas and skills, and I will be updating this section either yearly or bi-yearly to track my most recent projects.
